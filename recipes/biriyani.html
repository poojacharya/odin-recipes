Biriyani

Description

Mutton biryani is a flavorful, layered rice dish made with tender pieces of mutton (goat or lamb), fragrant basmati rice, and aromatic spices. It's a popular dish known for its rich, complex flavors, often cooked in a "dum" style with the meat and rice layered and slow-cooked in a sealed pot.

Ingredients

  • 750 g mutton (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 cups basmati rice (aged)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt (curd)
  • 1½ tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup chopped mint leaves (plus extra for layering)
  • ¼ cup chopped coriander leaves (plus extra for layering)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 3 medium onions, thinly sliced (for birista/fried onions)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • A few saffron strands soaked in 3 tbsp warm milk
  • 1 tbsp rose water (optional)
  • 1 tbsp kewra water (optional)
  • Whole spices for rice: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 2 green cardamoms, 1 black cardamom, 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Oil for deep frying (for onions)

Steps

  1. Wash and pat dry the mutton pieces. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, lemon juice, salt, mint, coriander, green chilies, and 2 tablespoons of fried onions. Add the mutton to this marinade and mix well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2–4 hours, preferably overnight.
  2. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Boil about 6–8 cups of water in a large pot. Add salt and the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon). Once it comes to a rolling boil, add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done (the grain should still have a bite). Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. To make fried onions (birista), thinly slice the onions and deep-fry them in hot oil over medium heat until golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain on paper towels. Set aside.
  4. In a heavy-bottomed pot or handi, spread the marinated mutton evenly at the bottom as the first layer.
  5. Add a layer of parboiled rice on top of the mutton. Sprinkle a generous amount of fried onions, some chopped mint and coriander, saffron milk, rose water, kewra water, and ghee over the rice. You can repeat layers if using a larger pot.
  6. Seal the pot tightly with aluminum foil or dough, then cover with a heavy lid. For stovetop cooking, place the sealed pot on a tawa (flat griddle) and cook over low heat for 40–45 minutes. Alternatively, bake in a preheated oven at 170°C (340°F) for 45–50 minutes.
  7. Once done, let the biryani rest for 10–15 minutes before opening. Gently mix from the bottom while serving to get all the layers.
  8. Serve hot with onion raita, cucumber salad, boiled eggs, or Hyderabadi mirchi ka salan.
